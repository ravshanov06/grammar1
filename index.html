📘 PERFECT TENSES ESL lab

⏳ Present Perfect

Simple have/has + past participle
I have visited London.
Unfinished time, life experience, result now.
Continuous have/has been + -ing
She has been studying for hours.
Action started past, still continuing or just stopped.

⏮️ Past Perfect

Simple had + past participle
They had left before I arrived.
Action completed before another past moment.
Continuous had been + -ing
He had been working all day.
Duration before something in the past.

🔮 Future Perfect

Simple will have + past participle
By June, she will have graduated.
Completion before a future time.
Continuous will have been + -ing
Next year I will have been living here for a decade.
Duration up to a future point.
